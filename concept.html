What We're Celebrating

❋

When two worlds meet, something is produced that neither could have made alone.

✦

There is a concept in Latin American thought called Mestizaje Creador — Creative Synthesis — articulated by the writer Arturo Uslar Pietri. It is a celebration of what happens when different cultures, histories, and ways of seeing the world come into contact with one another. The result is generative: something entirely new and native to nowhere.

Uslar Pietri argued that Latin America's greatest contribution to the world lay in its originality — a place where Indigenous, Spanish, and African people converged to produce a culture wholly its own. He called this the "Third Reality" — born when distinct paths meet and create something none could have produced alone.

The meeting of different worlds is generative. Through the convergance that bridges the distance between them, something is produced that carries its own vitality, its own texture, its own way of being.

❋

This is something we have lived. When we met, we knew we'd grown up a world apart, but we recognized each other instantly. The same values, the same things that brought us joy, the same pull toward community, toward exploration, toward revelry. It was effortless. From there, we took a gamble. Our future wasn't guaranteed, precisely because we were born a world apart, and forces beyond our control made every step uncertain. But we leaned into learning and shaping each other's worlds, teaching each other new ways of celebrating, of dancing, of marking occasions, of building home. Something grew in that exchange that feels like both of us — and is entirely beyond what either of us are alone.

This wedding will follow the same spirit — on purpose and by accident. The Bengali ceremony. The Goan setting. The music we'll dance to, the performances we're planning, the vibe, the guests traveling from across the world. We set out to design a wedding that would be meaningful to us, and this is what that looks like. A room full of people with rich backgrounds, from wildly different eras of our lives, ready to have a good time together — and we are so excited to watch it come to life.

Uslar Pietri believed that when different worlds meet, what emerges carries a universal quality — a kind of understanding that comes from having lived inside more than one story. We believe that too. It holds for families, for friendships, for any community where more than one world is represented.

✦

That is what we are celebrating this weekend. A marriage — and the powerful, unique experiences that emerge when people from different worlds open themselves to each other and choose to create something new.

❋ ✦ ❋
