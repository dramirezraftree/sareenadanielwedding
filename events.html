The Weekend

Wedding Events

Five celebrations across two days in Goa — each with its own palette and spirit.

Day One
Mehendi
Friday Afternoon · November 27
🕐 Afternoon

🎨 Temple Garden Pastels

Mehendi is how the weekend begins — an afternoon gathering where intricate henna designs are applied to the bride's hands and feet. Guests who'd like henna applied are welcome to join in too. In Bengali tradition, the darkness of the henna on the bride is said to reflect the depth of the groom's love. It's a relaxed and festive opportunity for everyone to settle into the weekend together.

Sangeet means "music together," and that's more or less what it is. In most Indian weddings, the Sangeet is an evening of performances — songs, dances, and routines put together by family and friends from both sides. Traditionally an intimate gathering of women singing folk compositions, it has evolved into a high-energy affair. Expect performances, toasts, and a lot of dancing — you know how we roll.

🎨 Sunlit Citrus

The Haldi — or gaye holud, meaning "yellow on the body" — is one of the most intimate rituals of a Bengali wedding. A paste of turmeric, sandalwood, and rose water is applied to the bride and groom by family and friends, a blessing rooted in the belief that turmeric purifies and protects. It will get messy. Wear something you're comfortable in — within the citrus palette — and don't expect to leave clean.

Wedding Ceremony
Saturday Sunset · November 28

The Bengali wedding ceremony — Biye — is built from rituals that have been practiced for centuries, each carrying its own significance. The bride's entrance begins with the shubho drishti, a sacred first look between the couple. The ceremony moves through the saptapadi, seven vows taken together, and culminates in the sindur daan — the moment the groom places vermilion in the part of the bride's hair. A pandit guides the ceremony through Sanskrit mantras, with fire, flowers, and conch shells woven throughout.

The Reception follows the ceremony and will be familiar to you. It's a chance to celebrate, eat well, and spend the evening with the people who made the trip. In Bengali tradition, the priti bhoj is a feast that formally welcomes the bride into her new family — it is a meaningful ceremony where the bride and groom's families sit together to share supper.
