The Weekend

Wedding Events

Five celebrations across two days in Goa — each with its own spirit and traditions.

Day One
Mehendi
Friday Afternoon · November 27
🕐 Afternoon
📍 TBD · The Windflower Resort
🎨 Temple Garden Pastels

Mehendi is how the weekend begins — a relaxed afternoon as everyone arrives and gets settled. The bride will have her henna applied, a ritual that is considered auspicious, a way of marking the occasion and blessing the bride before the ceremonies begin. Guests who'd like henna are welcome to join in. Beyond that, it's an easy afternoon — lunch, the pool, getting to know the resort and each other. If you arrive before check-in, come hang out with us.

Sangeet
Friday Evening · November 27
🕗 Evening
📍 TBD · The Windflower Resort
🎨 Ocean Jewel Tones

Sangeet translates roughly to "music together," and it describes how we'll spend the evening. Friends and family from both sides will perform choreographed dances and give speeches. Sareena and Daniel will too. The Sangeet began as an intimate tradition of women singing folk songs in the days before a wedding; it has evolved into a high-energy, festive celebration. We will have a DJ, lots of dancing, and try our best not to overdo it.

Day Two
Haldi
Saturday Morning · November 28
🕙 Morning
📍 TBD · The Windflower Resort
🎨 Sunlit Citrus

The Haldi — gaye holud in Bengali, meaning "yellow on the body" — is a particularly important ceremony in Bengali tradition. After breakfast, family and friends apply a paste of turmeric, sandalwood, and rose water to the bride and groom. It provides purification and protection — blessing the couple and warding off evil spirits. It will get messy (go easy on Daniel, please). After the ceremony, we'll spend time hanging out by the pool until the bride and groom disappear to get ready.

Wedding Ceremony
Saturday Sunset · November 28
🕔 Sunset
📍 TBD · The Windflower Resort
🎨 Coral Temple Sunset

The Bengali ceremony — Biye — is built from rituals practiced for centuries. The bride's entrance begins with the shubho drishti, a sacred first look. The ceremony moves through the saptapadi, where the couple will take seven steps together around a sacred fire and make seven promises: nourishment, strength, prosperity, happiness, family, health, and friendship. It culminates in the sindur daan, the moment the groom places vermilion in the part of the bride's hair. A pandit guides everything through Sanskrit mantras. Light snacks and refreshments will be offered during the ceremony.

Reception
Saturday Evening · November 28
🕗 Evening
📍 TBD · The Windflower Resort

The Reception is the closing note — a chance to eat well, linger, and be together one last time before the weekend ends. In Bengali tradition, the priti bhoj is a feast that formally welcomes the bride into her new family, the two sides sitting down together to share a meal. That spirit is what we're after. It's a moment to celebrate a new beginning and let the ceremonies wind down.
