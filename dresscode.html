Woven Light

Dress Code

Our wedding weekend follows a color progression inspired by the way light moves through a Goan day — from the soft pastels of a tropical garden in the afternoon, through the jewel-toned shimmer of ocean light at dusk, to the warm glow of a coastal sunset and finally the quiet elegance of moonlight on the water. Each event has its own palette and dress spirit, from casual to formal.


Indian and western attire are both warmly welcome at every event. Sarees, lehengas, kurtas, sherwanis, anarkalis, suits, dresses — whatever makes you feel festive and beautiful. The color palettes below are inspiration, not rules. Because several events are outdoors on the lawn, we recommend lightweight fabrics and shoes suited for warm weather and grass.

A Few Final Notes

Indian & Western Both Welcome

We mean this for every event — sarees, lehengas, sherwanis, anarkalis, kurtas, suits, gowns, dresses. There is no wrong choice as long as it fits the spirit of the occasion and the color palette. We want everyone to feel like themselves.

Fabrics

Silk, brocade, linen, chiffon, and breathable natural fabrics are ideal. Metallic threads, beadwork, and embroidery catch the light beautifully — lean into textures that shimmer.

Footwear

Some events are on the lawn and some are indoors — bring a mix of footwear that makes you feel good and suits what you're wearing. Sandals, juttis, heels, wedges — all welcome.

The Spirit

Vibrant, luminous, joyful. The palettes are inspiration — interpret them creatively. If you wear something colorful and festive that makes you feel wonderful, you've got it exactly right.
