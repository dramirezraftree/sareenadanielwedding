The Basics
When and where is the wedding?

The wedding weekend takes place on Friday, November 27th and Saturday, November 28th, 2026 at The Windflower Resort & Spa in Varca, South Goa, India. Checkout is Sunday, November 29th.

Can I bring a plus-one?

Plus-ones will be specified in your invitation. If you have any questions about your invitation, feel free to reach out to us directly.

Do you have a registry?

Yes! You can find our registry on the Registry page — it'll take you straight to our Zola. Thank you so much for thinking of us.

Do I need a visa to visit India?

Yes, most international visitors will need a visa to enter India. The good news is that India's e-Visa is straightforward to apply for online and is available to citizens of most countries. We recommend applying at least a month before your travel date — check the application window requirements for your nationality and don't leave it too late. Check indianvisaonline.gov.in for the latest requirements based on your nationality.

Travel & Getting There
Which airport should I fly into?

It depends on when you're arriving. If you're coming directly for the wedding weekend, fly into Dabolim Airport (GOI) in South Goa — it's about 25–35 minutes from the venue. If you're joining us early for the pre-wedding days in North Goa (November 24–26), fly into Mopa Airport (GOX) instead. However, you can fly into either airport and should choose a flight path that works best for your itinerary. See the Travel & Accommodations page for more detail.

How do I get from the airport to the venue?

Uber operates in Goa and is the easiest option — just request a ride on arrival. Taxis are also available at both airports. We'll share more details closer to the date and may coordinate some group transfers depending on arrival times.

What's the best way to get around Goa during the trip?

Uber and local taxis cover most needs. For the wedding weekend itself, everything is at the resort so you won't need much. More practical tips on the Travel & Accommodations page.

We have a block of rooms reserved at The Windflower Resort for the wedding nights — Friday, November 27th and Saturday, November 28th (checkout Sunday, November 29th). These rooms are covered — guests staying within the block don't need to book or pay. If you arrive at the venue on Thursday evening, you are welcome to book an additional night at the Windflower or at a nearby hotel.

Practical Matters
What's the weather like in Goa in November?

November is one of the best times to visit Goa. The monsoon is over, it's warm and sunny with temperatures around 28–32°C (82–90°F), and humidity is low. Pack light, breathable fabrics and sunscreen.

What currency is used and should I carry cash?

India uses the Indian Rupee (INR). Cards are accepted at most hotels and restaurants, but it's worth carrying some cash for local markets, auto-rickshaws, and tips. ATMs are available at the airport and in town for you to withdraw rupees directly, but there may not be an ATM at the resort. The best way to avoid withdrawal fees is to bring cash to exchange for rupees, but we do not recommend exchanging currency at the airport currency exchanges — you can often find better deals at exchanges in the city.

How do I get a SIM card or mobile data?

Indian SIM cards are easy to pick up at the airport with a passport — Airtel and Jio both offer good coverage in Goa. International roaming works too. We've also heard of friends who have gotten good deals and good coverage through digital SIMs procured through apps.
